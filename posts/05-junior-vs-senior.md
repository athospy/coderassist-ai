## Hook

Junior devs: stop waiting to "master the basics" before using AI. Senior devs: stop ignoring AI because you already know how to code.

## Caption

Two traps I see constantly in 2026.

The first is the junior developer who plans to learn AI tools after they have mastered the fundamentals. The problem is that AI tools are now part of the fundamentals. Waiting means falling behind before you start.

The second is the senior developer who does not need AI because they already know the codebase. The problem is that the engineer next to them is shipping twice as fast and spending their saved time on the work that actually gets noticed.

The right approach looks different depending on where you are.

If you are early in your career: use AI as a learning accelerator. Ask it to explain the code it writes. Build understanding and speed at the same time. The goal is not to skip the learning — it is to compress it.

If you are experienced: use AI for the parts of the job that drain your energy without requiring your judgment. Boilerplate, tests, documentation, first drafts. Free your brain for architecture and the decisions only you can make.

AI does not replace experience. It multiplies it — if you let it.

Where were you six months ago on this? 👇
