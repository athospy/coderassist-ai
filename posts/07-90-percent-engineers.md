## Hook

Gartner predicts 90% of engineers will shift from writing code to orchestrating AI agents by 2026. Is that a demotion or a promotion?

## Caption

The prediction sounds alarming until you think about what it actually means.

Writing code was never the goal. Solving problems was. Code was just the most direct way to do it.

If AI can write the code — and it increasingly can — then the engineer's value moves up the stack. It moves into system design, judgment, problem decomposition, and the ability to tell when the AI is confidently wrong.

That is not a lesser job. It is a more strategic one.

The engineers who will struggle are the ones who built their identity around the act of writing code. The engineers who will thrive are the ones who understood that writing code was a means, not the end.

The skills that rise in importance:
→ Knowing what to build and what not to build.
→ Designing systems that AI can extend without breaking.
→ Reviewing AI-generated output with genuine rigor.
→ Translating ambiguous business problems into precise tasks.

AI gives you leverage. Experience decides what you do with it.
