## Hook

## Caption

The way most developers use Claude Code:

Ask a question. Get an answer. Ask the next question. Repeat until the feature works or the context window runs out.

This produces mediocre output and a lot of back-and-forth rewriting.

Here is what works better:

Before asking for any code, describe the full picture first. Share the relevant parts of the codebase structure. Explain what you are trying to build and why. Then say: "Ask me any clarifying questions before you start."

What happens next is different. The AI surfaces assumptions you had not considered. It asks about edge cases before writing around them. It understands your architecture instead of inventing one. The output fits — not because the AI is smarter, but because it had enough context to act like a collaborator instead of a search engine.

The mental model that makes this click: treat Claude Code like a smart junior developer on their first day. They need orientation before they can contribute. Give them orientation.

Try this on your next feature. The rewriting drops significantly.
